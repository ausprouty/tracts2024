Have you heard of the Four Spiritual Laws? O Sa Bau Rogoca Li Na Lawa E Va Ni Bula Vakayalo?
Just as there are physical laws that govern the physical universe, so are there spiritual laws that govern your relationship with God. Me vaka ga na kena tu na Lawa e lewai kina na vuravura vakayago, e tu talega na lawa vakayalo e lewai kina na nomudrau veimaliwai kei na Kalou.
1

LAW ONE

God LOVES you and offers a wonderful PLAN for your life.

1

MATAI NI LAWA

E lomani iko na Kalou ka sa tu oti na nona I NAKI me baleta na nomu bula.

(References contained in this booklet should be read in context from the Bible whenever possible) (Na vei tiki ni Vola Tabu kece era cavuti e na i vola lailai oqo me ra vakayagataki me vaka ga na kena vakaibalebaletaki e na vanua e kune kina e na i Vola Tabu)
God's Love NA LOLOMA NI KALOU
"For God so loved the world that He gave His one and only Son, that whoever believes in Him shall not perish but have eternal life." (John 3:16, NIV) “Ni sa lomani ira na kai vuravura vakaoqo na Kalou, me solia kina na Luvena e duabau ga ka vakatubura, me kakua ni rusa e dua vei ira sa vakabauti Koya, me ra rawata ga na bula tawa mudu. (Joni 3:16)
God's Plan NA NONA I NAKI NA KALOU
[Christ speaking] “I came that they might have life, and might have it abundantly” [that it might be full and meaningful]. (John 10:10) [E vosa tiko na Karisito] “Au sa lako mai me rawa na bula, ia, me rawa vakalevu sara” [me vakaibalebale vakalevu]. (Joni 10:10)
Why is it that most people are not experiencing the abundant life? Because... A cava na vuna era sega ni vakila tu kina na bula vakaibalebale vakalevu, se bula e taucoko e vuqa na tamata? Baleta…
2

LAW TWO

People are SINFUL and SEPARATED from God so we cannot know and experience God’s love and plan for our life.

2

BALETA KARUA NI LAWA

Ni tamata ei valavala ca ka tawasei tu mai vua na Kalou. O koya gona e sega kina ni rawa ni vakila se tovolea na loloma ni Kalou kei na i naki ni Kalou me baleta na nona bula.

People are Sinful NA TAMATA E I VALAVALA CA.
"All have sinned and fall short of the glory of God." (Romans 3:23) “Ni sa i valavala ca ko ira kecega na tamata ka ra sega ni yacova na ka e vakarokorokotaki kina na Kalou.” (Roma 3:23)
We were created to have a personal relationship with God, but by our own choice and self-will we have gone our own independent way and that relationship has been broken. This self-will, often seen as an attitude of active rebellion towards God or a lack of interest in Him, is an evidence of what the Bible calls sin. E a buli na tamata me veiwekani vata kei na Kalou; ia, e na vuku ni nona yalo be ni lewai koya ga vakai koya, sa lewa kina me lakova ga na nona gaunisala vaka i koya, sa yaco me muduki kina na nodrau veiwekani kei na Kalou. Na yalo ni lewai koya vaka i koya, ka vakaraitaki Sara ga ena veisaqati, se sega ni via kauwaitaka na dina, a i vakadinadina ni ka e vakatoka na i Vola Tabu na i valavala ca.
People are Separated E TAWASEI TU NA TAMATA
"The wages of sin is death" [spiritual separation from God]. (Romans 6:23) “Ni sa i sau ni valavala ca na mate” [Veitawasei vakayalo kei na Kalou]. Roma 6:23
This picture illustrates that God is holy and people are sinful. A great gap separates the two. The arrows illustrate that people are continually trying to reach God and the abundant life through their own efforts, such as a good life, philosophy, or religion – but they always fail. Na Kalou e savasava ka tawa cala; ia, na tamata e i valavala ca. Ka tawasei rau e dua na maliwa levu. E saga tiko na tamata e na veigauna me yacova na Kalou kei na bula taucoko ka vakaibalebale vakalevu, ena nona sasaga ga vaka i koya; bula dodonu, i tovo vinaka veivakasama vuku se titobu eso, kei na so tale na nona vei sasaga.
The third law explains the only way to bridge this gap… Nai katolu ni lawa e solia vei keda na i wali dua dua ga ni leqa oqo…
3

LAW THREE

Jesus Christ is God’s ONLY provision for our sin. Through Him alone you can know and experience God’s love and plan for your life.

3

KATOLU NI LAWA

Ko Jisu Karisto duadua ga e vakarautaka na Kalou me baleta na i valavala ca ni tamata. E na vukuna, ko na rawa ni kila ka tovolea kina na loloma ni Kalou kei na nona i naki me baleta na nomu bula.

He Died in Our Place SA KEDA I SOSOMI E NA MATE E DODONU ME DA A MATE KINA
"God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us." (Romans 5:8) "Ia na KaIou sa vakatakila na nona loloma Ievu vei keda, ni sa mate na Karisito me keda i sosomi ni da sa tamata ca.” (Roma 5:8)
He Rose From the Dead SA TUCAKE TALE MAi NA MATE
"Christ died for our sins…He was buried…He was raised on the third day, according to the Scriptures…He appeared to Peter, then to the Twelve. After that He appeared to more than five hundred…" (1 Corinthians 15:3-6) “Sa mate na Karisito e na vuku ni noda i valavala ca. Sa bulu…. Sa tucake tale e na i ka tolu ni siga me vaka nai Vola Tabu…. Sa rairai vei Pita, kei iratou na lewe tinikarua. Ni oti ko ya, sa rairai vei ira era sivia e lima na drau.” (1 Koronica 15:3‑6)
He Is the Only Way to God 0 KOYA DUADUA GA NA SALA
"Jesus said to him, 'I am the way, and the truth, and the life; no one comes to the Father but through Me.'" (John 14:6) “Sa kaya vua ko Jisu: ‘Ko i au na sala, kei na dina kei na bula; sa sega ni torovi Tamaqu walega e dua na tamata e na vukuqu ga.” (Joni 14:6)
This diagram illustrates that God has bridged the gulf that separates us from Him by sending His Son, Jesus Christ, to die on the cross in our place to pay the penalty for our sins. Na Kalou sa vakaikawakawataka na mal'wa ka tawasei keda tu mai vua, e na nona tala mai na Luvena, ko Jisu Karisito me keda i sosomi, e na nona mate ena kauveilatai.
It is not enough just to know these three laws... E sega nI rauta na noda klla se vakasamataka wale tu ga na lawa e tolu oqo…
4

LAW FOUR

We must individually RECEIVE Jesus Christ as Saviour and Lord; then we can know and experience God’s love and plan for our lives.

4

KA VA NI LAWA

Me da dui ciqomi Jisu Karisito yadudua me noda turaga ka i vakabula. Eda na qai rawa ni kila ka tovolea kina na loloma ni Kalou kei na nona i naki me baleta na nona bula.

We Must Receive Christ E DODONU ME DA CIQOMI KARISITO
"As many as received Him, to them He gave the right to become children of God, even to those who believe in His name." (John 1:12) "Ia, ko ira kecega sa vakabauti Koya, sa solia vei ira ko Koya me ra yaco rawa me ra luve ni Kalou vei ira sa vakabauta na yacana." (Joni 1:12)
We Receive Christ Through Faith EDA NA RAWA WALEGA NI CIQOMI KARISITO E NA VAKABAUTA
"By grace you have been saved through faith; and that not of yourselves, it is the gift of God; not as a result of works so that no one may boast." (Ephesians 2:8,9) “Ni sa loloma walega dou sa vakabulai kina e na vuku ni vakabauta; ia, sa sega ni vuna ko i kemudou; ai solisoli walega ni Kalou. Sa sega e na vuku ni cakacaka, me kakua ni dua me dokai koya.” (Efeso 2:8‑9)
We Receive Christ by Personal Invitation EDA CiQOMI KARISITO E NA NODA SURETI KOYA YADUDUA VAKA I KEDA KINA NODA BULA.
[Christ is speaking] “Behold, I stand at the door and knock; if any one hears My voice and opens the door, I will come in to him.” (Revelation 3:20) [E vosa tiko na Karisito] “Raica ka’u sa tu e na mata ni katuba, ka tukituki; kevaka sa rogoca na domoqu e dua na tamata, ka dolava na katuba, au na curu vua.” (Vakatakila 3:20)
Receiving Christ involves turning to God from self (repentance) and trusting Christ to come into our lives to forgive our sins and to make us what He wants us to be. Just to agree intellectually that Jesus Christ is the Son of God and that He died on the cross for our sins is not enough. Nor is it enough to have an emotional experience. We receive Jesus Christ by faith, as an act of the will. Na ciqomi Karisito sa kena i balebale na noda biuta na lomada ka vakanamata vua na Kalou, ka da nuitaki Karisito me lako mai ki na noda bula, me bokoca na noda i valavala Ca, ka buli keda me da vaka na tamata e vinakata. E sega ni rauta na vakaio walega ni noda vakasoma ki na Nona lewa, se na noda vakila ga e lomada.

You Can Receive Christ Right Now by Faith Through Prayer E NA RAWA NI KO CIQOMI KARISITO SARAGA E NA GAUNA OQO E NA MASU
(Prayer is talking with God) (Na masu na veivosaki vata kei na Kalou)
God knows your heart and is not so concerned with your words as He is with the attitude of your heart. The following is a suggested prayer: Na Kalou e kila vinaka na yalomu, ka sega soti ni kauwai e na nomu vosa, ia, e na i tovo ga ni gagadre ni yalomu. Oqo e dua na masu ni vakabauta me vukea na nomu vakatulewa:
Lord Jesus, I need You. Thank you for dying on the cross for my sins. I open the door of my life and receive You as my Savior and Lord. Thank You for forgiving my sins and giving me eternal life. Take control of the throne of my life. Make me the kind of person You want me to be. “Turaga Jisu, au gadrevi Kemuni. Au sa dolava na katuba ni noqu bula, kau sa ciqomi Kemuni me noqu Turaga ka i Vakabula. Vinaka vakalevu na nomuni bokoca na noqu ni valavala ca. Ni lewa mada mai nai tikotiko vakatui ni noqu bula. Ni buli au me’u yaco me vaka na tamata ko ni vinakata” – Emeni.
Does this prayer express the desire of your heart? If it does, I invite you to pray this prayer right now. E vakaraitaka li na gagadre ni yalomu na masu oqo?
How to Know That Christ Is in Your Life KO NA KILA VAKAEVEi NI SA TIKO NA KARISITO E NA NOMU BULA
Did you receive Christ into your life? According to His promise in Revelation 3:20, where is Christ right now in relation to you? Christ said that He would come into your life. Would He mislead you? On what authority do you know that God has answered your prayer? (The trustworthiness of God Himself and His Word, the Bible.) Ko sa ciqomi Karisito oti kina nomu bula? Me vaka na nona vosa ni yalayala e na i Vakatakila 3:20, evei na vanua e sa tu kina na Karisito e na gauna oqo ena nomudrau veimaliwai? E tukuna na Karisito ni na curu mai kina nomu bula. E na rawa beka ni liutaki iko vakatani? Ko kila vakaevei ni sa sauma na Kalou na nomu masu? (Na nona rawa ga ni nuitaki na Kalou vaka i Koya kei na Nona Vosa)
The Bible promises Eternal Life to all who receive Christ. NAI VOLA TABU E YALATAKA NA BULA TAWA MUDU VEI IRA KECE ERA CIQOMI KARISITO
"God has given us eternal life, and this life is in His Son. He who has the Son has the life; he who does not have the Son of God does not have the life. These things I have written to you who believe in the name of the Son of God, so that you may know that you have eternal life." (1 John 5:11-13) “Oqo ga nai tukutuku ni sa solia vei keda na Kalou na bula Tawa Mudu, ia, na bula oqo sa tu vua na Luvena. 0 koya sa tu vua na Luvena, sa tu vei koya na bula; 0 koya sa sega ni tu vua na Luve ni Kalou, sa sega ni tu vua na bula. A veika oqo kau sa vola vei kemudou mo dou kila kina ni dou sa rawata na bula tawa mudu, io, vei kemudou sa vakabauta na yaca ni Luve ni Kalou.” (1 Joni 5:11‑13)
Thank God often that Christ is in your life and that He will never leave you (Hebrews 13:5). You can know on the basis of His promise that Christ lives in you and that you have eternal life from the very moment you invite Him in. He will not deceive you. Vakavinavinaka wasoma vua na Kalou ni sa tiko e na nomu bula (Iperiu 13:5). Sa rawa ni ko kila na Karisito bula ni sa tiko e lomamu, ka ko sa rawata na bula tawa mudu, mai na gauna saraga ko sa sureti Koya kina me vaka na nona vosa ni yalayala. E na sega ni rawa ni vakaisini iko na Kalou.
What about feelings? E vakaevei na nomu vakila a lomamu?
Do Not rely on your Feelings KUA NI VAKATAU KI NA NOMU VAKILA E LOMAMU
The promise of God's Word, the Bible - not our feelings - is our authority. The Christian lives by faith (trust) in the trustworthiness of God Himself and His Word. This train diagram illustrates the relationship among fact (God and His Word), faith (our trust in God and His Word), and feeling (the result of our faith and obedience). (Read John 14:21) Na vosa ni yalayala ni Kalou me da vakatau kina, ka segai e na noda vakila e lomada. Na tamata Lotu Vakarisito, e bula lako e na Vakabauta (Vakadinata) ni rawa ni vakadinati na Kalou vakaikoya kei na Nona Vosa. Na droini ni sitima ni vanua ka koto oqo e ra e vakaraitaki kina na nodratou semati vata na Ka Dina (Kalou kei na Nona Vosa), Na Vakabauta (Na noda vakadinata na Kalou kei na nona vosa), kei na Vakila (Na ka e yaco e na noda Vakabauta kei na noda Vakarorogo) (Joni 14:21).
The train will run with or without a passenger car. However, it would be useless to attempt to pull the train by the passenger car. In the same way, as Christians we do not depend on feelings or emotions to decide what is true, but we place our faith (trust) in the trustworthiness of God and the promises of His Word. E na rawa ni cici vata kei na kena qiqi se cici duaduaga vakataki koya na sitima ni vanua. Ia, e na ka lialia kevaka e via dreta se cicivaka na sitima ni vanua na qiqi. E vaka talega kina koi keda na tamata Lotu. Eda sega ni vakatau ki na noda vakila ena lomada, ia, eda vakotora na noda vakabauta (vakadinata) e na nona dina ka rawa ni nuitaka na Kalou kei na ka e yalataka na Nona Vosa.
Now That You Have Received Christ IA OQO NI KO SA CIQOMI KARISITO OTI
The moment you received Christ by faith, as an act of the will, many things happened, including the following: Mai na gauna ko sa ciqomi Karisito kina a ena vakabauta, e vuqa na ka sa yaco vei iko, ka okati kina na veika oqo:
  • Christ came into your life (Revelation 3:20 and Colossians 1:27)
  • Sa lako oti mai na Karisito ki na nomu bula. (Vakatakila 3:20 kei na Kolosa 1:27)
  • Your sins were forgiven (Colossians 1:14)
  • Na nomu i valavala ca sa bokoci oti. (Kolosa 1:14)
  • You became a child of God (John 1:12)
  • Ko sa yaco mo luve ni Kalou. (Joni 1:12)
  • You began the great adventure for which God created you. (John 10:10; 2 Corinthians 5:17; 1 Thessalonians 5:18)
  • Ko sa tekivutaka nai lakolako ka a buli iko kina na Kalou. (Joni 10:10; 2 Koronica 5:17; 1 Cesalonaika 5:18)
Can you think of anything more wonderful that could happen to you than receiving Christ? Would you like to thank God right now for what He has done for you? By thanking God, you demonstrate your faith. Ko rawa ni vakasamataka e dua na ka e na uasivi cake mai na nomu ciqomi Karisito ki na nomu bula? Ko vinakata mo vakavinavinaka vua na Kalou e na masu e na vuku ni ka sa vakayacora Oti vei iko? Na nomu vakavinavinaka oqo vua na Kalou e i vakaraitaki ni nomu vakabauta.

Suggestions for Christian Growth NA VEIKA ESO E RAWA NI VUKEI KINA NA TUBUCAKE VAKARISITO
Spiritual growth results from trusting Jesus Christ. "...The righteous man shall live by faith" (Galatians 3:11). A life of faith will enable you to trust God increasingly with every detail of your life, and to practice the following: Na tubucake vakayalo, e vu mai na vakararavi vei Jisu Karisito “E na bula na tamata yalo dodonu e na vakabauta” (Kalatia 3:11) Na bula ni vakabauta e na vukei iko me tubucake kina na nomu vakararavi vua na Kalou e na veitikina matailalai ni nomu bula ka mo cakava na veika oqo:
  • Go to God in prayer daily (John 15:7)
  • Lako vua na Kalou e na masu a na veisiga. (Joni 15:7)
  • Read God's Word daily. (see Acts 17:11) Begin with the Gospel of John.
  • Wilika na vosa ni Kalou a na veisiga. (Cakacaka 17:11) tekivu e na Kosipeli e vola ko Joni
  • Obey God moment by moment. (see John 14:21)
  • Vakarorogo vua na Kalou e na vei tiki ni gauna kece sara. (Joni 14:21)
  • Witness for Christ by your life and words. (see Matthew 4:19 and John 15:8)
  • Vakadinadinataki Karisito ena nomu bula kei na nomu vosa. (Maciu 4:19; Jon 15:8)
  • Meet regularly with other believers to encourage them to love and good deeds (Hebrews 10:25).
  • E dodonu me da lewena e dua nai vavakoso Lotu se dau lotu e na dua na vale ni lotu, ka vakarokorokotaki kina na Kalou. (Iperiu 10:25)
  • Holy Spirit – allow Him to control and empower your daily life and witness (Galatians 5:16,17; Acts 1:8).
  • Vakararavi vei Karisito e na vei gacagaca matai lalai ni nomu bula e na veisiga kei na nomu vakadinadinataka e na nomu bula. (Kalatia 5:16‑17; Cakacaka 1:8)
Fellowship in a Good Church E KA BIBI ME DUA NA I VAVAKOSO LOTU VINAKA
The Bible tells us the importance of meeting together with other Christians (Hebrews 10:25).
Have you ever watched a fire burning in a fireplace? Several logs together burn brightly. But if you pull one log away from the fire, its flame soon goes out. The same thing happens to us if we do not spend time with other Christians. Attend a church where Christ is talked about and the Bible is taught. Start this week and make plans to attend regularly. 		E na iperiu 10:25 eda sa vakaroti kina "me da kskua ni bluta na vakasoqoni vata me vaka na i valavala ni tamata e so..." Era na waqa vakavinaka na veitiki ni buka ke ra uturaki vata, ia, ni kau tani e dua mai dravu ka vakotori taudua, e nd boko. Sa vaka kina na noda veimaliwai kei ira na tamata Lotu Vakarisito tale e so. Ke ko sega ni lewena tiko e dua na i vavakoso lotu, kua tale ni qai wnwa mo sureti. Liu sara ga na nomu lako vakai iko ki vua na kena i talatala se o kaya e liutaka tiko na lotu, e na i vavakoso lotu e dokai kina na Karisito ka vunautaki kina na nona Vosa. Tekivu e na macawa oqo, ka sasaga mo dau lako e na kena gauna dodonu.
