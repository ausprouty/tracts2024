Have You Heard of the Four Spiritual Laws? Chear atmik kaide hanche vixim tuvem aikolam?
There are natural laws that control the world in which we live. There are also spiritual laws which determine your relationship with God. Zoxe porim soimbache kaide asat je soimbik samball-tat, tech porim atmik kaide asat je Deva ani tujea mo-dem soirigot samballtat.
1

Law One

God LOVES you and offers a wonderful PLAN for your life.

“God so loved the world that He gave His one and only Son, that whoever believes in Him shall not perish, but have eternal life” (John 3:16 NIV). Devan sonvsaracho itlo mog kelo ki Tannem Aplea ekleach Putak vopun dilo, Tacher visvas thevtat team soglleancho nas zainam zauncheak, punn tankam sasnnachem jivit mellcheak. (Juanv 3:16)
GOD’S PLAN DEVACHI EUZONN
[Christ speaking] “I came that they might have life, and might have it abundantly” (John 10:10). (Krist mhonnta) "Hanv ailam to tankam jivit melloun diunk ani tem tankam subhamponnan melloun diunk ailam." (Jea vorvim tem jivit sompurnn ani orth aslelem zaunk) (Juanv 10:10)
Why aren’t all people experiencing the abundant life? Choddxea lokak subham jivitacho onnubhov kiteak nam?
2

Law Two

Man is SINFUL and SEPARATED from God. Thus, he cannot know and experience God’s love and plan for his life.]

2

DUSRO KAIDO

MONIS PATKI ZAUN ASA ANI DEVASORXILO KODDSORLOLO ASA, ANI HEACH KHATIR DEVACHEA MOGACHI OLLOKH ANI APLEA JIVITANT DEVACHI EUZONN KITEM, HANCHO TAKA ONNUBHOV VO SOMZONNI MELLONAM.

MAN IS SINFUL MONIS PATKI
“All have sinned and fall short of the glory of God” (Romans 3:23). Khorench soglle patki zaleat ani tankam Devachem onondbhorit dorxon ontorlam. (Romkarank 3:23)
Man was created to have fellowship with God; but, because of his stubborn self-will, he chose to go his own independent way, and fellowship with God was broken. This self-will, characterized by an attitude of active rebellion or passive indifference, is an evidence of what the Bible calls sin. Devachea ektarant ranvche khatiruch Devan monxeak rochlo, punn gorvan ani ap-khoxen to apleak zai te porim cholunk laglo, ani he porim Deva ani tache modem aslolo ektar tuttlo. Hi ap-khoxi, jiche ogunn akromonn ani kanbhere zaun asat, tika Povitr Postuh patok mhonn pacharta.
MAN IS SEPARATED MONIS DOXIN ZALO
“The wages of sin is death” [spiritual separation from God] (Romans 6:23). Patok dita to muxaro moron. (mhonnge Deva ani monxea modem atmik futt) (Romkarank 6:23)
God is holy and man is sinful. There is a great separation between the two. The arrows show how man tries to reach God and the abundant life through his own efforts: such as living a good life, being religious, helping others etc., but all his efforts fail. Dev povitr ani monis patki. Tanche modem ek bhov rund khonn asa; Boreo kornneo, dhorm, xan-neponn, zanvai, adi, osle aple upai vapddun monis aplech ostongim Devathaim ani sompurnn jivitak pavunk tambonastanna vavurta.
The third law gives the only answer to this problem… Hea proxnna-voir Tisro Kaido ekuch zabab dita...
3

Law Three

Jesus Christ is God’s ONLY provision for man’s sin. Through Him you can know and experience God’s love and plan for your life.

3

TISRO KAIDO

MONXEACHEAM PATKAM KHATIR FOKOT JEZU KRIST DEVACHI EKUCH BHET ZAUN ASA. TACHE VORVIM DEVACHEA MOGA-VIXIM ANI TUJEA JIVITANT DEVA-CHE EUZONNE VIXIM TUJEAN SOMZONNI ANI ONNUBHOV GHEUNK ZATA.

HE DIED IN OUR PLACE AMCHE BOLDEK TO MELO
“God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us.” (Romans 5:8) Amche sorim Apnnak asa to mog Devan dakhoilo, tor ami patki asleanv tednam pasun, Krist amche pasot melo. (Romkarank 5:8)
HE ROSE FROM THE DEAD TO MELELEANTLO JIVONT ZALO
“Christ died for our sins...He was buried...He was raised on the third day, according to the Scriptures...He appeared to Peter, then to the twelve. After that He appeared to more than five hundred…” (1 Corinthians 15:3-6). Krist amcheam patkam pasun melo...Taka nikhipilo, Povitr Pustokam pormonnem tisrea disa To jivont zalo....Kefasak ani uprant barank to dixtti poddlo. Tea uprant, ekach farak, painxim odik bhavank dixtti Poddlo... ( 1 Kor. 15:3-6)
HE IS THE ONLY WAY TO EKUCH VATT ZAUN ASA
“Jesus said to him, ‘I am the way, and the truth, and the life; no one comes to the Father, but through Me.’” (John 14:6) Jezun taka zap dili, 'Vatt, Sot, ani Jivit Hanvanch kon-nunch Bapa-Xim vochonam bogor Mhoje voraim.' (Juanv 14:6)
God has bridged this separation between us and Himself by sending His Son Jesus Christ, to die on the cross in our place. Amche boldek Aple Putak, Jezu Kristak, khursar morunk diun Devan Aple ani amche modem aslolo vegllozar som poun porot ektar ghoddlo.
It is not enough just to know these three laws … He tin kaide fokot zanna zalear puro mhoi....
4

Law Four

We must individually RECEIVE Jesus Christ as Saviour and Lord; then we can know and experience God’s love and plan for our lives.

4

CHOUTO KAIDO

AMI ZONN-EKLEAN JEZU KRISTAK APLO SODDVONNAR ANI DHONI MHONN MANUN GHEUNK ZAI. TEDNANCH AMCHEAN DEVACHEA MOGA-VIXIM ANI AMCHEA JIVITANT DEVACHE EUZONNE-VIXIM SOMZONNI ANI ONNUBHOV GHEUNK ZATA.

WE MUST RECEIVE CHRIST AMI KRISTAK MANUN GHEUNK ZAI
“As many as received Him, to them He gave the right to become children of God, even to those who believe in His name” (John 1:12) Punn Apnnak manun ghetleleam somestank, Aplea nanvacher visvas thevteleank, Devachim bhurgim zaunk Tannem podvi dili. (Juanv 1:12)
WE MUST RECEIVE CHRIST AMI JEZU KRISTAK BHAVARTA-UDEXIM MANUN GHETANV
“By grace you have been saved through faith; and that not of yourselves, it is the gift of God; not as a result of works, that no one should boast” (Ephesians 2:8,9). Denneak lagun bhavarta udexim tumkam soddvonn mell'li, ani tumche osttongim nhoi punn Devache funkott kurpek lagun. Hea kamant ami mijas korum noie, kiteak amchi soddvonn amcheam kornniam vorvim upzonam (Efezkarank 2:8,9)
WE MUST RECEIVE CHRIST THROUGH FAITH (Read John 3:1-8) AMI KRISTAK MANUN GHETOCH AMKAM NOVEA ZOLMANCHO ONNUBHOV MELLTA. (Juanv 3:1-8 vach)
WE RECEIVE CHRIST THROUGH PERSONAL INVITATION KHAS AMI KRISTAK APOUN-UNCH TAKA MANUN GHETANV
[Christ speaking] “Behold, I stand at the door and knock; if any one hears My voice and opens the door, I will come in to him.” (Revelation 3:20) (Krist mhonnta): "Ho polle Hanv darachea umbrear asam ani darar martam; Mhozo tallo aikun konn Mhaka dar ugttem korit tachea ghorant Hanv bhitor sortolom." (Prokasnni 3:20)
Receiving Christ involves turning to God from self (repentance) and trusting Christ to come into our lives to forgive our sins and to make us what He wants us to be. Kristak manun ghetolo zalear apleakuch fatt korunk zai ani Devak fuddo korunk zai (mhonge dukh dhorunk zai) ani Krist amchea jivitant ietolo amchim patkam bogxitolo ani Taka zai tosIe monis amkam kortolo mhonn ami bhorvanso dhorunk zai.
Just to agree intellectually that Jesus Christ is the Son of God and that He died on the cross for our sins is not enough. Nor is it enough to have an emotional experience. We receive Jesus Christ by faith, as an act of the will. Jezu Krist Devacho Put ani To amcheam patkam khatir khursar melo oxem mhonn ami fokot amche zanvaienunch manun ghetlear puro mhoi. Nhoi mhonn amkam vonddavonnicho onnubhov meulearuch puro nhoi. Ami bhavartan Kristak manun gheunk zai; ani hi amche khoxechi kornni zaun asteli.

YOU CAN RECEIVE CHRIST RIGHT NOW BY FAITH THROUGH PRAYER BHAVARTAN MAGNNEM KORUN TUJEAN ATANCH KRISTAK MANUN GHEUNK ZATA
(Prayer is talking to God) (Magnnem mhonnge Deva-thaim ulovop zaun asa)
God knows your heart and is not so concerned with your words as He is with the attitude of your heart. The following is a suggested prayer: Dev tujem kalliz ollkhota, ani To tujim utram pollenam punn To tujea kallzache otreg polleta. Fuddem asa tem suchoilelem bhavartachem magnnem tujean mhonnum-ieta.
“Lord Jesus, I need You. I have been controlling my own life and have sinned against You. Thank You for forgiving my sins. Come into my life and take control of my life. Make me the kind of person You want me to be.” Dhonia Jezu, Tuji maka goroz. Mhojea jivitachem dar hanv ugoddtam ani Tuka mhozo Soddvonnar ani Dhoni mhonn manun ghetam. Mhojim patkam bhogxilim mhonn Tuje upkar attoitam. Mhojea jivitachi sodri Tum ghe. Tuka zai toslo monis maka kor. Amen.
Does this prayer express the desire of your heart? Hem magnnem tujea kallzache anvdde dakoll korta?
If it does, I invite you to pray this prayer right now, and Christ will come into your life, as He promised. Hoi zalear, atanch hem magnnem kor ani Aple bhasavnne pormonnem Krist tujea jivitant ietolo.
HOW TO KNOW THAT CHRIST IS IN YOUR LIFE KRIST TUJEA JIVITANT ASA MHONN TUJEAN KOXE-PORIM ZANNA-ZAUNK ZATA?
Did you receive Christ into your life? According to His promise in Revelation 3:20, where is Christ right now in relation to you? Christ said that He would come into your life. Would He mislead you? On what authority do you know that God has answered your prayer? (The trustworthiness of God Himself and His Word.) Tuvem magnem kelem tednam tuvem Kristak tujea jivitant apoilolo? Tor Prokasnni 3:20 hantunt dilele bhasavnne pormonnem Krist hea vellar tuje sombondim khoim asa? Apun tujea jivitant ietolo mhonn kristan bhasailam. Tor To tuke fottoita? (Devan tuiem magnnem aikilem mhonn tuka koxem gomta?) Devan tujea magnneank zabab dilo mhonn tuka koxem gomta? (Khas Dev ani Tachem Utor, Povitr Pustok, Visvaxik dekun; dusream utramnim, Dev Apleo bhasavnneo samballta.)
THE BIBLE PROMISES ETERNAL LIFE TO ALL WHO RECEIVE CHRIST ZO KONN KRISTAK MANUN GHETA TAKA POVITR PUSTOK SASNNACHEM JIVIT BHASAITA.
“The witness is this, that God has given us eternal life, and this life is in His Son. He who has the Son has the life; he who does not have the Son of God does not have life. These things I have written to you who believe in the name of the Son of God, in order that you may know that you have eternal life” (1 John 5:11-13). Hi asa ti govaiki: Devan amkam sasnnachem jivit dilam, Ani hem jivit Tachea Puta sovem asa. Jea mon'xa sovem put asa, tea mon'xak Jivit mell'lam: jea mon'xa sovem Put nam, tea mon'xa sovem jivit-ui nam. Tamche sovem sasnnik jivit asa, kiteak tumin Devachea Putacher visvas dovorla; hem tumi zanno zauncheak, itlem hanv tumkam boroitam. (1 Juanv 5: 11-13)
Thank God often that Christ is in your life and that He will never leave you (Hebrews 13:5). You can know that Christ lives in you and that you have eternal life because of His promise. Krist tujea jivitant asa mhonn, ani To tuka kedinch sanddchonam dekun Devak sodanch argam dhi. (Hebrev 13:5). Tuvem Kristak apoilo tech golliek, Tache bhasavnne pormonnem, jivo Krist tujea kallzant riglo mhonn ani tuka sasnnachem jivit meulem mhonn tuka qomtelem. To tuka fottouncho-nam
An important reminder… Vhonddavonn-ache vixim kitem?
DO NOT DEPEND ON FEELINGS VHONDDAVONN-ACHER VISVAS DOVORUNK FAVONAM
The promise of God’s Word, the Bible - not our feelings - is our authority. The Christian lives by faith (trust) in the trustworthiness of God Himself and His Word. Amchi vhonddavonn nhoi, fokot Devachea Utrachi bhasavnni amcho odhikar zaun asa. Kristi monis Devachea visvasponnacher ani Tachea Utracher patieun jieta. Hem ghaddiechem chitr Sotacho, bhavartacho, ani vhonddavonnecho ektar dokoita.
This train diagram illustrates the relationship among fact (God and His Word), faith (our trust in God and His Word), and feeling (the result of our faith and obedience). SOT - Dev ani Tachem Utor. BHAVART - Devacher ani Tachea Utracher amchem patiennem. VHONDDAVONN - Amchea bhavartcho ani pall-dinneacho porinam.
The train will run with or without a guard's van. However, it would be useless to attempt to pull the train by the guard's van. In the same way, as Christians we do not depend on feelings or emotions, but we place our faith (trust) in the trustworthiness of God and the promises of His Word. Provaxi-ancho dobo aslearui vo naslearui ghaddi cholta. Provaxianchea dobean ghaddiek vhoddunk tozvit kelear ti vhoddon ieunchinam Tech porim ami Kristanvanim amcheam vhonddavonneancher visvas dovorunk favonam, punn amcho bhavart (patiennem) Devachea visvasponnacher ani Tachea utranche bhasavnnecher ami dovorunk zai.
NOW THAT YOU HAVE RECEIVED CHRIST ATAM JEDNAM TUVEM KRISTAK MANUN GHETLO
The moment that you received Christ by faith, as an act of the will, many things happened, including the following: Je ghoddiek tum bhavartan Kristak manun gheta, tednam zaitem ghoddta, khala dokoilam tem dhorun:
1. Christ came into your life (Revelation 3:20, Colossians 1:27) 1. Tujea jivitant Krist eilo. (Prokasnni 3:20, ani Koloskar 1:27)
2. Your sins were forgiven (Colossians 1:14). 2. Tujim patkam bhogxilim. (koloskar 1:14)
3. You became a child of God (John 1:12). 3. Tum Devacho bhurgo zalo. (Juanv 1:12)
4. You began the great adventure for which God created you (John 10:10, 2 Corinthians 5:14, 15, 17). 4. Tuvem ek kherit jivitachi vatt tankunk suru kelem ani heach khatir Devan tuka rochlolo. (Juanv 10:10; 2 Korintkar 5:17; ani 1 Tesalonkar 5:18)
Can you think of anything more wonderful that could happen to you than receiving Christ? Would you like to thank God in prayer right now for what He has done for you? By thanking God, you demonstrate your faith. Kristak manun ghetoch jivitant ji ek ojeapanchi ghoddni ghoddta tache poros odik ojeapanchi vost tujean dakhounk zata? Devan tujem jivit boldilem dukun heach vellar magnneam-vorvim Devak argam diunk tuka khoxi asa? 'Devak argam divop' hench kortub monxeacho bhavart dakoita.
What next? Tor atam kitem?
SUGGESTIONS FOR CHRISTIAN GROWTH KRISTI JIVITANT VADDONK SUCHOVNNEO
Spiritual growth results from trusting Jesus Christ. Jezu Kristacher patielearuch Atmik jivit vaddta; "Nitivont monis bhavartan jietolo" (Galatikar 3:11). Bhavartan jielear tujea jivitant dor-eka kortubant odik ani odik Devacher patieun raunk zatelem; ani he porim tujean oxen korunk zatelem:
1. Go to God in prayer daily (John 15:7). V - Voch dispottim Deva-thaim ani magnnem kor (Juanv 15:7).
2. Read God’s Word daily (Acts 17:11); begin with the Gospel of John. A - Aik Devachem Utor dispottim (Dhormdutancho Ithias 17:11) - Juancho grant than suru kor.
3. Obey God moment by moment (John 14:21). D - Devachem Utor ghollieche-gholliek pall (Juanv 14:21)
4. Witness for Christ by your life and words (Matthew 4:19; John 15:8). D - Dhi govaiki Krista Khatir tujeam utramnim ani kortubamnim (Matev 4:19 Juanv 15:8)
5. Trust God for every detail of your life (1 Peter 5:7). A - Akhea tujea jivitant dor-eka kortubant Devacher patie (1 Pedru 5:7)
6. Have God control and empower your daily life and witness (Galatians 5:16 & Acts 1:8). I - Itlean tujem dispottem jivit ani govaiki samballunk ani tim soktevont zaunk tum tukach povitr Atmeachea tabeant kor (Galatikar 5:16,17; Dhormdutancho Ithias 1:8)
FELLOWSHIP IN A GOOD CHURCH Kristi jivitant vaddcheak anik ek bhov ghorjeachi vost asa
When logs are together; they burn brightly. Put one aside on the cold ground and the fire will begin to go out. Zaitim lakdam ekttaim aslear khoraien zolltat;punn tantlem ek kuxin kaddlear tem palouta.
It is important that you should join other people who have received Jesus Christ as their Lord and Saviour. You should go to a Church where people believe that the Bible is true and where Jesus Christ is obeyed. (Hebrews 10:25) Jesus Kristak aplo Dhoni ani Soddvonddar mhunn manun ghetleleancho sangat zoddop khup gorjechem toxench ji sobha Povitr Pustok khorem mhunn manta and Jezu Kristachem utor palltat.
