Do you have peace with G-d?האם יש לך שלום עם אלוהים?
The following four facts explain how it is possible to know the G-d of Avraham, Yitzchak, and Ya’acov. G-d Himself has provided the way through the Holy Scriptures.
1

First Fact

The G-d of Avraham, Yitzchak, and Ya’acov loves you. He created you for a specific purpose, that you might know Him in a close and intimate way and enjoy His peace forever.
THE LOVE OF G-D

PEACE WITH G-D
For I know the plans that I have for you,” declares the Lord, “plans for welfare and not for calamity to give you a future and a hope. (Jeremiah 29:11) כִּי אָנֹכִי יָדַעְתּי אֶת-הַמַּחֲשָׁבֹת אֲשֶׁר אָנֹכִי חֹשֵׁב עֲלֵיכֶם נְאֻם-ה' מַחְשְׁבוֹת שָׁלוֹם וְלֹא לְרָעָה לָתֵת לָכֶם אַחֲרִית וְתִקְוָה (ירמיה כט: יא)‏‏
Why then, do only a few experience this love, joy and peace with G-d through a close personal relationship with Him?
2

Second Fact

Man is sinful and separated from G-d. Therefore he cannot enjoy the love of G-d and a personal relationship with Him.
People are Sinful
Indeed, there is not a righteous man on earth who continually does good and who never sins. (Ecclesiastes 7:20) כִּי אָדָם אֵין צַדִּיק בָּאָרֶץ אֲשֶׁר יַעֲשֶׂה-טּוֹב וְלֹא יֶחֱטָא (קהלת ז: כ)‏‏
Man was created to have fellowship with G-d. But, in his stubbornness, man chose to go his own way and therefore this fellowship with his creator was severed. This stubbornness, characterised by active rebellion or passive indifference, is evidence of what the Bible calls “sin”.
MAN IS SEPARATED FROM G-D
Behold, the Lord’s hand is not so short that it cannot save; neither is His ear so dull that it cannot hear. But your iniquities have made a separation between you and your G-d, and your sins have hidden His face from you, so that He does not hear. (Isaiah 59:1-2) הֵן לֹא-קָצְרָה יַד-ה' מֵהוֹשִׁיעַ וְלֹא-כָבְדָה אָזְנוֹ מִשְּׁמוֹעַ: כִּי אִם-עֲוֹנֹתֵיכֶם הָיוּ מַבְדִּלִים בֵּינֵכֶם לְבֵין אֱלֹהֵיכֶם וְחַטֹּאתֵיכֶם הִסְתִּירוּ פַנִים מִכֶּם מִשְּׁמוֹעַ (ישעיה נט: א–ב)‏‏

WE CANNOT BRIDGE THE GAP THROUGH OUR OWN GOOD WORKS.
For all of us have become like one who is unclean, and all our righteous deeds are like a filthy garment; and all of us wither like a leaf, and our iniquities, like the wind, take us away. (Isaiah 64:5 [Isaiah 64:6 in English]) וַנְּהִי כַטָּמֵא כֻּלָּנוּ וּכְבֶגֶד עִדִּים כָּל-צִדְקֹתֵינוּ וַנָּבֶל כֶּעָלֶה כֻּלָּנוּ וַעֲוֹנֵנוּ כָּרוּחַ יִשָּׂאֻנוּ (ישעיה סד: ה)‏‏
The third fact explains what is the one and only way to bridge this gap.
3

Third Fact

The atoning blood of the Meshiach is the only way that G-d has provided to solve the problem of man’s sin. Only by means of the Meshiach’s sacrifice are you able to experience a personal relationship with G-d and a satisfying and peaceful life.

IDENTIFYING G-D’S MESHIACH

• From the lineage of the house of David. ( Micah 5:1; 5:2 in English)
• His supernatural character. (Isaiah 9:5-6)
• His death on a cross. (Isaiah 53:9-10)
• His resurrection from the dead. (Isaiah 53:12, Psalms 16:10)
• His death before the second destruction of the temple, that was destroyed in 70 CE. (Daniel 9:26)
There is only one man in history who fulfilled all these prophecies …

Fourth Fact

As the children of Israel trusted in the atoning sacrifices (Leviticus 16:21), we are to trust in a personal way in the atonement of Yeshua the Meshiach, so that through it we experience the love of G-d and a personal and intimate relationship with Him.
HOW DO WE RECEIVE YESHUA AS THE MESHIACH?
He calls us to turn personally to Him
(Yeshua is speaking) Behold I stand at the door and knock If anyone hears My voice and opens the door, I will come in to him and will dine with him and he with Me. (Revelation 3:20) "(ישוע אומר‏) הִנְנִי עוֹמֵד לְיַד הַדֶּלֶת וְדוֹפֵק. אִישׁ כִּי יִשְׁמַע אֶת קוֹלִי וְיִפְתַּח אֶת הַדֶּלֶת, אֶכָּנֵס אֵלָיו וְאֶסְעַד אִתּוֹ וְהוּא אִתִּי.‏‏
Receiving the Meshiach, Yeshua, involves turning from our ways to G-d (repentance) and trusting that Yeshua the Meshiach will enter our lives, forgive our sins and restore the severed relationship between us and G-d. There are people who think that Jewish people who receive the Meshiach are no longer Jewish. This thinking is incorrect. This person is no less Jewish than before; on the contrary, he is a person who has devoted himself to the Jewish Meshiach.

What about your feelings?
Do Not rely on your Feelings
Salvation is based on G-d’s promised Word - the Bible – not on your personal feelings. The follower of Yeshua lives by faith (trust) in the trustworthiness of G-d Himself and His Word. This train diagram illustrates the relationship between fact (G-d and His Word), faith (our trust in G-d and His Word), and feeling (the result of our faith and obedience). (John 14:21).
The train will run with or without a passenger car. However, it would be useless to attempt to pull the train by the passenger car. In the same way, as believers we do not depend on feelings or emotions, but we place our faith (trust) in the trustworthiness of G-d and the promises of His holy Word.

SUGGESTIONS FOR SPIRITUAL GROWTH
Spiritual growth results from trusting the Meshiach. “The righteous man shall live by faith” (Galatians 3:11). A life of faith will enable you to trust G-d increasingly with every detail of your life and to practice the following:
1. Go to G-d in prayer daily. (John 15:7)
2. Read G-d's Word, the Bible, daily. (Acts 17:11) Begin with the Gospel of John.
3. Obey G-d, moment by moment. (John 14:21)
4. Witness for the Meshiach by your life and words. (Mathew 4:19, John 15:8)
5. Trust G-d for every detail of your life. (1 Peter 5:7)
6. Allow the Holy Spirit to control and empower your daily life and witness. (Galatians 5:16, 17 and Acts 1:8)
THE IMPORTANCE OF THE CONGREGATION
In Hebrews 10:25, we are encouraged to meet regularly with other believers. Several logs burn brightly together; but put one aside from the burning flames and the fire in the log goes out. So is it with your relationship to other believers. If you do not belong to a local fellowship of believers, do not wait to be invited. Take the initiative; contact or visit a nearby congregation where the Meshiach is honored and His Word is preached. Start this week, and make plans to attend regularly.
