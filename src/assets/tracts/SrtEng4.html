Have you heard of the Four Spiritual Laws?A O Kwele Ka Melao E Mene Ya Moya?
Just as there are physical laws that govern the physical universe, so are there spiritual laws that govern your relationship with God.Bjalo ka ge go na le melao eo e laolago tlholo yohle, go na le melao ya semoya eo e leolago tswalano ya rena le Modimo.
1

God LOVES you and offers a wonderful PLAN for your life.

God's Love LERATO LA MODIMO
“God so loved the world that He gave His one and only Son, that whoever believes in Him shall not perish, but have eternal life” (John 3:16 NIV). “Gobane Modimo o ratile lefase gakaakaa, a ba a le nea Morwa wa gagwe yo a tswetšwego a nnoši gore mang le mang e a mo dumetšego go yena a se ke a lahlega, a be le bophelo bjo bo sa felego.” (Johane 3:16)
God's Plan LEANO LA MODIMO
Jesus said, "I came that they may have life, and have it abundantly" [that it might be full and meaningful]." (John 10:10) [Go bolela Kriste] “Se nna ke se tletšego ke gore le be le bophelo le mokhora.” (Johane 10:10)
Why is it that most people are not experiencing the abundant life? Because... Ke ka baka la eng ge batho ba bantši ba sa phele bophelo bjo bo tletšego?

2

People are SINFUL and SEPARATED from God so we cannot know and experience God’s love and plan for our life.

 2

Motho ke MODIRADIBE, gape o AROGANE le Modimo. Ka baka leo o hlaela go tseba le go itemogila lerato le leano la Modimo ka bophelo bja gago.
People are Sinful MOTHO KE MODIRADIBE
"All have sinned and fall short of the glory of God." (Romans 3:23) “Gobane moo ga go tlhaolo, ba dirile dibe bohle, ba hlaya letago la Modimo.” (Baroma 3:23)
We were created to have a personal relationship with God, but by our own choice and self-will we have gone our own independent way and that relationship has been broken. This self-will, often seen as an attitude of active rebellion towards God or a lack of interest in Him, is an evidence of what the Bible calls sin. Motho o bopetšwe go ba le setswalle le Modimo; eupša, ka baka la go se kwe, o ile a ikgethela tsela ya gagwe, gomme setswalle sa gagwe le Modimo se senyega. Go se kwe ga gagwe go laetšwa ke go se ikokobetše pele ga Modimo, ešita le go hlokomologa Modimo, gomme se ke bohlatse bja seo Bibele e se bitšago sebe.
People are Separated MOTHO O AROGANE LE MODIMO
"The wages of sin is death" [spiritual separation from God]. (Romans 6:23) “Gobane moputso wa sebe ke lehu” [karogano le Modimo ka moya] (Baroma 6:23)
This picture illustrates that God is holy and people are sinful. A great gap separates the two. The arrows illustrate that people are continually trying to reach God and the abundant life through their own efforts, such as a good life, philosophy, or religion – but they always fail. Tshwantšho ye e bontšha gore Modimo ke yo mogwetha, mola motho e le modiradibe. Sekgala se segolo se arogantšha motho le Modimo. Metšu e bontšha gore motho o tšama a leka go iphihliša go Modimo le bophelong bjo bo tletšego. O leka ka maatla ka go phela bophelo bjo botse, ka go ithuta le ka go direla Modimo - eupša tšohle tše di a pala.
The third law explains the only way to bridge this gulf… Molao wa boraro o re fa tharollo ya bothata bjo…
3

Jesus Christ is God’s ONLY provision for our sin. Through Him alone you can know and experience God’s love and plan for your life.

 3

Jesu Kriste ke yena FELA tharollo ya Modimo ya bothata bja sebe sa motho. Ka yena o ka tseba, gomme wa itemogela lerato la Modimo le leano la gagwe ka bophelo bja gago.
HE DIED IN OUR PLACE O HWILE BAKENG SA RENA
“God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us” (Romans 5:8). “Gomme Modimo, lerato le a re ratilego ka lona, o le bonagatša ka ge Kriste a re hwela re sa le badiradibe.” (Baroma 5:8)
HE ROSE FROM THE DEAD O TSOGILE BAHUNG
"Christ died for our sins…He was buried…He was raised on the third day, according to the Scriptures…He appeared to Peter, then to the Twelve. After that He appeared to more than five hundred…" (1 Corinthians 15:3-6) “Kriste o hwile ka baka la dibe tša rena… O fihlilwe, a tsoga ka letšatši la boraro ka mo mangwalo a bolelago. Gomme o bonwe ke Kefase, a tla a bonwa ke ba lesome le metšo e medebi. A buša a bonwa ke bana bešo ba ba makgolo a mahlano…” (1Bakorinthe 15:3-6).
He Is the Only Way to God KE YENA FELA TSELA YA GO YA MODIMONG
"Jesus said to him, 'I am the way, and the truth, and the life; no one comes to the Father but through Me.'" (John 14:6) “Jesu a re: ‘Tsela ke nna, le therešo, le bophelo. Ga go e a tlago go Tate ge e se ka nna.'” (Johane 14:6)
This diagram illustrates that God has bridged the gulf that separates us from Him by sending His Son, Jesus Christ, to die on the cross in our place to pay the penalty for our sins. Tšhwantšho ye e bontšha gore Modimo o tswaletše sekgala seo se re arogantšhago le yena ka go romela Morwa wa gagwe, Jesu Kriste, go re hwela sefapanong. Ka go dira bjalo, a re lefela dibe tša rena.
It is not enough just to know these three laws... Ga go a lekana go no tseba melao ye meraro ye…
4

We must individually RECEIVE Jesus Christ as Saviour and Lord; then we can know and experience God’s love and plan for our lives.

 4

Rena ka o tee ka o tee, re swanetše go AMOGELA Jesu Kriste bjalo ka Mophološi le Morena. Gona re tla tsebe gape ra itmogela lerato la Modimo le leano la gagwe maphelo a rena.
WE MUST RECEIVE CHRIST RE SWANETŠE GO AMOGELA KRISTE
"As many as received Him, to them He gave the right to become children of God, even to those who believe in His name." (John 1:12) “Fela, bohle ba ba mo amogetšego o ba neile maatla a go ba bana ba Modimo. Ke bao ba dumetšego leina la gagwe.” (Johane 1:12)
We Receive Christ Through Faith RE AMOGELA KRISTE KA TUMELO
"By grace you have been saved through faith; and that not of yourselves, it is the gift of God; not as a result of works so that no one may boast." (Ephesians 2:8,9) “Le baphološwa ka kgaugelo ka tumelo; ga la ka la iphološa, e fo ba mpho ya Modimo. Ga la ka la e bona ka mediro, gore go se be le e a kago itumiša.” (Baefeso 2:8,9)
When we receive Christ we experience a new birth. (Read John 3:1-8) GE RE AMOGELA KRISTE, RE TSWALWA LEFSA (Bala Johane 3:1-8)
We Receive Christ by Personal Invitation RE AMOGELA KRISTE KA GOMO LALETŠA
[Christ speaking] "Behold, I stand at the door and knock; if anyone hears My voice and opens the door, I will come in to him." (Revelation 3:20) [Go bolela Kriste] “O tsebe gore ke eme mojako, ke a opaopa. Ge motho a ekwa lentšu la ka mme a tula mojako, ke tlo tsena go yena ka lalela naye, yena a lalela le nna.” (Kutollo 3:20)
Receiving Christ involves turning to God from self (repentance) and trusting Christ to come into our lives to forgive our sins and to make us what He wants us to be. Just to agree intellectually that Jesus Christ is the Son of God and that He died on the cross for our sins is not enough. Nor is it enough to have an emotional experience. We receive Jesus Christ by faith, as an act of the will. Re amogela Kriste ge re ipolela dibe ra gomela go Modimo, gomme ra tshepha Kriste gore a tsene maphelong a rena, a re swarele dibe tša rena, gomme a re dire seo a ratago gore re be sona. Go fo dumela ka tlhaologanyo gore Kriste ke morwa wa Modimo, le go re o re hweletše dibe tša rena mo sefapanong, ga go a lekana. Le gona, ga go a lekana go no huduega mo moyeng. Re amogela Kriste ka boikemišetšo le kholofelo.

Do Not rely on your FeelingsSE TSHEPHE MAIKUTLO
The promise of God's Word, the Bible - not our feelings - is our authority. The Christian lives by faith (trust) in the trustworthiness of God Himself and His Word. This train diagram illustrates the relationship among fact (God and His Word), faith (our trust in God and His Word), and feeling (the result of our faith and obedience). (Read John 14:21)Maatla a rena ke tshephišo ya Lenetšu la Modimo, e seng maikutlo. Mokriste o phela ka tumelo (tshepho) botshepheging bja Modimo ka boyena, gotee le Lentšu la gagwe. Seswantšho sa setimela se laetša kamano gare ga nnete (Modimo le lentšu la gagwe), tumelo (tshepho ya rena go Modimo le lentšu la gagwe), le maikutlwelo (poelo ya tumelo ya rena le go kwa). (Bala Johane 14:21)
The train will run with or without a passenger car. However, it would be useless to attempt to pull the train by the passenger car. In the same way, as Christians we do not depend on feelings or emotions to decide what is true, but we place our faith (trust) in the trustworthiness of God and the promises of His Word.Setimela se ka kitima ntle le bene ya mohlokomedi. Empa, e ka ba ka lefeela go leka go goga terene ka bene ya mohlokomedi. Empa e ka ba ka lefeela go leka go goga terene ka bene ya mohlokomedi. Ka tsela e bjalo, rena, bjalo ka baKriste, ga re itshetlege ka maikutlwelo goba khuduego ya moya; empa re bea tumelo (tshepho) botshepheging bja Modimo le ditshephišong tša lentšu la gagwe.

SUGGESTIONS FOR CHRISTIAN GROWTHDITŠHIŠINYO MABAPI LE GO GODIŠA BOKRISTE
Spiritual growth results from trusting Jesus Christ. "...The righteous man shall live by faith" (Galatians 3:11). A life of faith will enable you to trust God increasingly with every detail of your life, and to practice the following: Go gola ka tsela ya semoya go holwa ke ge Mokriste e tshepha Jesu Kriste. “Moloki o tlo phela ka tumelo” (Bagalatia 3:11). Bophelo bja tumelo bo tla go kgontšha go tshepha Modimo ka karolo tšohle tša bophelo bja gago le go phetha dilo tša go swana le tše:
  • Go to God in prayer daily (John 15:7)
  • Thapelo Go ya go Modimo ka thapelo ka mehla (Johane 15:7)
  • Read God's Word daily. (see Acts 17:11) Begin with the Gospel of John.
  • Go bala Go bala Lentšu la Modimo ka mehla (Ditiro 17:11); thomiša ka Ebangedi ya Johane.
  • Obey God moment by moment. (see John 14:21)
  • Boikokobetšo Go ikokokobetša pele ga Modimo, ka nako tšohle. (Johane 14:21)
  • Witness for Christ by your life and words. (see Matthew 4:19 and John 15:8)
  • Bohlatse Go hlatsela Kriste ka dipolelo le mesepelo ya gago. (Mateo 4:19; Johane 15:8)
  • Trust God for every detail of your life (1 Peter 5:7).
  • Tshepho Go tshepha Modimo ka karolo tšohle tša bophelo bja gago. (1 Peter 5:7)
  • Holy Spirit – allow Him to control and empower your daily life and witness (Galatians 5:16,17; Acts 1:8).
  • Boineelo Go dumela Moya o Mokgethwa go go laola le go nea maatla a go hlatsela Kriste (Bagalatia 5:16,17; Ditiro 1:8)
FELLOWSHIP IN A GOOD CHURCHSETSWALLE SA PHUTHEGO
The Bible tells us the importance of meeting together with other Christians (Hebrews 10:25).
Have you ever watched a fire burning in a fireplace? Several logs together burn brightly. But if you pull one log away from the fire, its flame soon goes out. The same thing happens to us if we do not spend time with other Christians. Attend a church where Christ is talked about and the Bible is taught. Start this week and make plans to attend regularly.		Go Baherebe 10:25, re eletšwa gore, “Re se ngale puthego ya gešo…” Dikgong tše ntši di tuka botse gammogo; empa ge serumula se sengwe se ka beelwa thoko, se a tima. Go bjalo le ka kopano ya gago le BaKriste ba bangwe. Ge o sa tsene kereke, se emele bangwe gore ba go laleletše. Tšea dikgato o ikopanye le moruti wa kereke ya kgauswi yeo mo go yona go hlomphiwago Kriste ge go rerwa Lentšu la Gagwe. Thoma beke yona ye, o ikopanye le phutego ka mehla.
