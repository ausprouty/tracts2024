Have you heard of the Four Spiritual Laws?Na o se o Utlwetse Ka Melao E Mene Ya Moya Na?
Just as there are physical laws that govern the physical universe, so are there spiritual laws that govern your relationship with God.Jwale ka ha ho na le melao ya tlhaho e laolang tlhaho, ho na le nnete ya moya e laolang tswalano ya hao le Modimo.
1

God LOVES you and offers a wonderful PLAN for your life.

 1

Modimo wa o RATA, mme o na le MORERO o makatsang ka bophelo ba hao.
(References contained in this booklet should be read in context from the Bible wherever possible.) (Ha ho kgonahala, bala ditemana tse qotsitsweng ho ya ka moo Bibele e bolelang ka teng.)
God's Love LERATO LA MODIMO
“God so loved the world that He gave His one and only Son, that whoever believes in Him shall not perish, but have eternal life” (John 3:16 NIV). "Hobane Modimo o ratile lefatshe hakalo, o bile wa le nea Mora wa hae ya tswetsweng a nnotshi; hore e mong le e mong ya dumelang ho yena, a se ke a timela, a mpe a be le bophelo bo sa feleng." (Johanne 3:16)
God's Plan MORERO WA MODIMO
Jesus said, "I came that they may have life, and have it abundantly" [that it might be full and meaningful]." (John 10:10) (Kreste o itse) "Ke tlile hore le be le bophelo, le bo bone ka ho fetisisa." (hore bophelo bo felle mme bo bolele ho hong ho batho) (Johanne 10:10).
Why is it that most people are not experiencing the abundant life? Because... Ke hobaneng ha batho ba bangata ba se na bophelo bona bo fetisisang na?
2

People are SINFUL and SEPARATED from God so we cannot know and experience God’s love and plan for our life.

 2

Motho ke MOETSADIBE mme o AROHANE le Modimo, ka lebaka lena o sitwa tseba le ho utlwa lerato la Modimo le morero wa oona ka bophelo ba hae.
People are Sinful MOTHO KE MOETSADIBE
"All have sinned and fall short of the glory of God." (Romans 3:23) "Hobane bohle ba sitilwe, mme ha ba tshwanelwe ke kganya ya Modimo." (Baroma 3:23)
We were created to have a personal relationship with God, but by our own choice and self-will we have gone our own independent way and that relationship has been broken. This self-will, often seen as an attitude of active rebellion towards God or a lack of interest in Him, is an evidence of what the Bible calls sin. Motho o bopetswe ho ba le setswalle le Modimo: empa ka lebaka la manganga le ho rata ho iketsetsa boithatelo, motho o ile a ikgethela ho inkela tselana ya hae e mo arohanyang le Modimo mme hona ha senya kopano ya hae le Modimo. Boithatelo bona ba motho bo totobatswang ke maikutlo a ho lwantsha Modimo le ho iphapanyetsa oona, ke ponahatso ya seo Bibele e se bitsang sebe.
People are Separated MOTHO O AROHANE
"The wages of sin is death" [spiritual separation from God]. (Romans 6:23) "Hobane moputso wa sebe ke lefu" [karahano ya moya wa hao le Modimo] (Baroma 6:23)
This picture illustrates that God is holy and people are sinful. A great gap separates the two. The arrows illustrate that people are continually trying to reach God and the abundant life through their own efforts, such as a good life, philosophy, or religion – but they always fail. Setswatsho sena se hlalosa hore Modimo wa halalela mme motho yena ke moetsadibe. Kgohlo a kgolo e arohanya motho wa moetsadibe le Modimo. Metsu e hlalosa hore motho o leka ka nako tsohle ho finyella Modimo le bophelo bo fetisisang ka tsela tsa hae, jwalo ka ho phela bophelo bo botle, filosifo, ho ba le tumelo, jwalo-jwalo; empa maiteko ohle a hae ha a thuse letho.
The third law explains the only way to bridge this gulf… Nnete ya boraro e hlalosa seo e leng leborokgo la ho tshela kgohlo…
3

Jesus Christ is God’s ONLY provision for our sin. Through Him alone you can know and experience God’s love and plan for your life.

 3

Jesu Kreste ke yena FEELA tharollo yeo Modimo o fanang ka yona mobapi le sebe sa motho. Ka yena o ka tseba mme wa ikutlwela lerato la Modimo le morero wa oona wa bophelo ba hao.
HE DIED IN OUR PLACE O SHWELE SEBAKENG SA RONA
“God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us” (Romans 5:8). "Empa moo Modimo a totisitseng lerato la oona ho rona teng, ke ka hore, re sa le baetsadibe, Kreste o bile a shwa sebakeng sa rona." (Baroma 5:8)
HE ROSE FROM THE DEAD O TSOHILE BAFUNG
"Christ died for our sins…He was buried…He was raised on the third day, according to the Scriptures…He appeared to Peter, then to the Twelve. After that He appeared to more than five hundred…" (1 Corinthians 15:3-6) "…Kreste o shwele ka baka la dibe tsa rona… le hore o ile a patwa, a ba a tshoha bafung ka tsatsi la boraro. O ile a iponahatsa ho Kefase, hamoraoho ba leshome le metso e mmedi. Kamarao ho moo a iponahatsu ka nako e lenngwe ho badumedi ba fetang makgolo a mahlano…" (Bakorinthe 15:3-6)
He Is the Only Way to God KE YENA FEELA TSELA?
"Jesus said to him, 'I am the way, and the truth, and the life; no one comes to the Father but through Me.'" (John 14:6) "Jesu a re ho yena: 'Ke nna tsela, le nnete, le bophelo; ha ho motho ya ka tlang ho Ntate, ha e se ka nna.'" (Johanne 14:6)
This diagram illustrates that God has bridged the gulf that separates us from Him by sending His Son, Jesus Christ, to die on the cross in our place to pay the penalty for our sins. Setswantsho sena se hlalosa hore ka ho rmela Jesu Kreste Mora wa oona, ho tla shwa sefapanong sebakeng sa rona, Modimo o entse borokgo bo tshelang kgohlo e neng e re arohanya le oona.
It is not enough just to know these three laws... Ha ho a lekana ho tseba feela nnete tsena tse tharo…
4

We must individually RECEIVE Jesus Christ as Saviour and Lord; then we can know and experience God’s love and plan for our lives.

 4

Re lokela ho AMOHELA Jesu Kreste jwalo ka Mopholosi le Morena, mong ka mong; mme re ka tseba le ho ikutlwela lerato la Modimo le morero wa oona ka maphelo a rona.
WE MUST RECEIVE CHRIST RE TSHWANETSE HO AMOHELA KRESTE
"As many as received Him, to them He gave the right to become children of God, even to those who believe in His name." (John 1:12) "Empa bohle ba le amohetseng, le ba neile tokelo ya ho ba bane ba Modimo, e leng ba dumelang lebitso la lona." (Johanne 1:12)
We Receive Christ Through Faith RE AMOHELA KRESTE KA HO MO MEMA, MONG KA MONG
"By grace you have been saved through faith; and that not of yourselves, it is the gift of God; not as a result of works so that no one may boast." (Ephesians 2:8,9) "Hobane le bolokehile ke mohau, ka tumelo; ha ho tswe ho lona, ke mpho ya Modimo; hase ka mesebetsi, ho tle ho se ke ha eba motho ya ithorisang." (Baefese 2:8,9)
We Receive Christ by Personal Invitation HA RE AMOHELA KRESTE, RE TSWALWA LA BOCHA
[Christ speaking] "Behold, I stand at the door and knock; if anyone hears My voice and opens the door, I will come in to him." (Revelation 3:20) [Kreste ha a bua re] "Bona, ke eme monyako, mme ke a kokota; ekare ha e mong a utlwa lentswe la ka, mme a bula monyako, ke tla kena ha hae." (Tshenolo 3:20).
Receiving Christ involves turning to God from self (repentance) and trusting Christ to come into our lives to forgive our sins and to make us what He wants us to be. Just to agree intellectually that Jesus Christ is the Son of God and that He died on the cross for our sins is not enough. Nor is it enough to have an emotional experience. We receive Jesus Christ by faith, as an act of the will. Ho amohela Kreste ho kenyeletsa ho tlohela ho itaola, ra boyela ho Modimo (tshokoloho), ho thsepa Kreste hore a kene maphelong a rona ho re tshwarela dibe tsa rona le ho re etsa seo a ratang hore re be sona. Ha ho a lekana ho amohela ka kelello feela hore Jesu Kreste ke Mora wa Modimo le ho re o shwele sefapanong bakeng sa rona. Hape, ha ho a lekana ho ba le maikutlo a itseng mabapi le sena. Re amohela Kreste ka tumelo, e le sesupo sa ho etsa ho ya ka ho ikgethela.

Do Not rely on your FeelingsO SE KE WA ITSHETLEHA HODIMA MAIKUTLO A HAO
The promise of God's Word, the Bible - not our feelings - is our authority. The Christian lives by faith (trust) in the trustworthiness of God Himself and His Word. This train diagram illustrates the relationship among fact (God and His Word), faith (our trust in God and His Word), and feeling (the result of our faith and obedience). (Read John 14:21)Re tshwanetse ho itshetleha hodima Lentswe la Modimo, e seng hodima maikutlo a rona. Mokreste o phela ka tumelo (tshepo) botshepehing ba Modimo le ba Lentswe la oona. Setshwantso sena sa terene se tshwantsa kamano dipakeng tsa nnete (Modimo le Lentswe la oona), tumelo (tshepo ya rona ho Modimo le Lentsweng la oona), le maikutlo (metswela ya tumelo le kutlo ya rona) (Johanne 14:21)
The train will run with or without a passenger car. However, it would be useless to attempt to pull the train by the passenger car. In the same way, as Christians we do not depend on feelings or emotions to decide what is true, but we place our faith (trust) in the trustworthiness of God and the promises of His Word.Terene e ka tsamaya e na le letoroko la ho qetela kapa e se na lona. Empa e ka ba ntho e se nang thuso ho leka ho hula terene ka letoroko la ho qetela. Ka tsela e jwalo feela, rona Bakreste, ha re itshetlehe hodima maikutlo a rona; empa re behile tumelo (tshepo) ya rona botshepehing ba Modimo le ditshepisong tsa Lentswe la oona.

SUGGESTIONS FOR CHRISTIAN GROWTHDIKELETSO MABAPI LE HO HOLA HA MOKRESTE
Spiritual growth results from trusting Jesus Christ. "...The righteous man shall live by faith" (Galatians 3:11). A life of faith will enable you to trust God increasingly with every detail of your life, and to practice the following: Ho hola ha moya ho tswalwa ke ho tshepa Jesu Kreste. "Ya lokileng o tla phela ke tumelo" (Bagalata 3:11). Bophelo ba tumelo bo tla o thusa hore o nne o tswelepele ho tshepa Modimo karolong e nngwe le e nngwe ya bophelo ba hao, le ho o thusa ho etsa tsena tse latelang ka mehla:
  • Go to God in prayer daily (John 15:7)
  • Atamela Modimo ka thapelo ka mehla. (Johanne 15:7)
  • Read God's Word daily. (see Acts 17:11) Begin with the Gospel of John.
  • Bala Lentswe la Modimo ka mehla (Diketso 17:11)… qala ka Evangedi ya Johanne.
  • Obey God moment by moment. (see John 14:21)
  • Utlwa Modimo ka motsotso o mong le o mong. (Johanne 14:21)
  • Witness for Christ by your life and words. (see Matthew 4:19 and John 15:8)
  • Eba paki ya Kreste ka bophelo ba hao le ka mantswe a hao. (Mattheu 4:19; Johanne 15:8)
  • Trust God for every detail of your life (1 Peter 5:7).
  • Tshepa Modimo mabapi le karolo e nngwe le e nngwe ya bophelo ba hao. (1 Petrose 5:7)
  • Holy Spirit – allow Him to control and empower your daily life and witness (Galatians 5:16,17; Acts 1:8).
  • Dumella Moya o Halalelang ho o tsamaisa le ho a nea matla a letsatsi le leng le le leng le matla a ho paka Kreste. (Bagalata 5:16,17; Diketso 1:8)
FELLOWSHIP IN A GOOD CHURCHBOHLOKWA BA KOPANO YA BAKRESTE
The Bible tells us the importance of meeting together with other Christians (Hebrews 10:25).
Have you ever watched a fire burning in a fireplace? Several logs together burn brightly. But if you pull one log away from the fire, its flame soon goes out. The same thing happens to us if we do not spend time with other Christians. Attend a church where Christ is talked about and the Bible is taught. Start this week and make plans to attend regularly.		Ho Baheberu 10:25 re eletswa hore "re se ke ra ngala diphutheho tsa rona,…re mpe re kgothatsane…." Dipatsi tse ngata di etsa kganare e kgolohadi ya mollo ha di besitswe mmoho, empa ha o ka wa nka patsi e le nngwe e tukang mme wa e beha leifong le batang, e tla phakisa ho tima. Ho jwalo le ka kopano ya hao le Bakreste ba bang. Haeba ha o kene kereke moo Kreste a hlonephuwang teng le moo Lentswe la hae le bolelwang teng, nka kgato ho kopana ka mehla le batho ba bang ba fumaneng Kreste le bona e le Mopholosi wa bona mme ba tseba lerato la hae.
